General Affairs Office

(I) Director of General Affairs

Extension: 130

Responsibilities: All affairs of the General Affairs Office.

(II) Section Chief of Purchase & Maintenance

  • Supervising procurement and verification cases for goods and projects under NT$150,000.
  • Campus environment and building maintenance, beautification, management, and protection.
  • Regular service procurement such as elevator maintenance, security, water quality testing, and weeding.
  • Periodic procurement and verification for septic tank pumping, vector mosquito disinfection, and water tower cleaning.
  • Loaning of school buildings and venues.
  • Management of janitorial staff.
  • Civil defense: organization of the protection corps.
  • Tendering, acceptance, settlement, and verification for service, property, and engineering procurement cases exceeding one-tenth of the announced amount.

(III) Officer of the Purchase & Maintenance Section

  • Daily work: Procurement and verification of goods, classification and registration of property (non-consumables and consumables), and labeling of property.
  • Regular work: Registration, custody, and distribution of property. Regular and irregular inventory of property. Handling of damaged and scrapped property. Requisition, usage registration, statistics, and custody of consumables.

(IV) Section Chief of Documentation

  • Duties such as receiving, sending, distributing, checking, filing, cataloging, archiving, and destroying official documents.
  • Duties such as compiling and recording materials for administrative meetings and school affairs meetings.
  • Assisting with the registration of incoming and outgoing mail and parcels.
  • Assisting with postage management and sending mail.
  • Other temporary assignments.

(V) Section Chief of Cashier

  • Payroll management system: Establishing basic data, payroll distribution, distribution of hourly fees for part-time and substitute teachers, distribution of various scholarships and subsidies, salary income declaration, and deduction of various insurance premiums.
  • Student registration management system: Establishing basic data, issuing registration forms, and accounting, statistics, and compilation of registration fees.
  • Cashier management system: Issuing receipts for various public funds and subsidies, issuing unified receipts for various incomes paid to the treasury. Issuing treasury checks based on vouchers for payment and collection operations. Preparing daily balance sheets. Reconciling accounts with the treasury and preparing discrepancy explanation forms.

(VI) Clerk of the General Affairs Office

  • Assisting the Documentation Section with receiving and sending mail and parcels, document processing, etc.
  • Assisting the Cashier Section with receiving and disbursing funds, creating registers, calculating supplementary insurance premiums, and managing petty cash.
  • Organizing and creating registers for travel expenses.
  • Other temporary assignments.

(VII) Electrical Technician of the General Affairs Office

  • Handling official documents and inspections for high-voltage electrical equipment, tracking electricity consumption, setting contract capacity, adjusting power factor, and controlling peak demand to save on electricity costs.
  • Filling out energy-saving website forms and promoting related business.
  • Cooperating with the General Services Section on the execution and planning of electricity-related business (estimation of electrical equipment lines, estimation of air conditioning capacity for installation, calculation of electricity fees for rented venues, etc.).
  • Cooperating with the Purchase & Maintenance Section on troubleshooting school-wide electrical equipment failures (including after-hours and during typhoons).
  • Assignments from superiors (including school-wide property management).

Counseling Office

(03)977-1131 #150 (Day School) / #171 (Continuing Education Division)
Direct Line: (03)977-4418
tcvs150@tcvs.ilc.edu.tw

(I) Career Counseling ~ Helping Students Reach for a Wider Sky:

  • Editing the "Heritage" handbook to provide students with a reference for further education interviews.
  • Providing career planning courses to guide students in planning their career blueprints and enhance their career development abilities.
  • Implementing interest tests and vocational aptitude tests to help students understand themselves and realize their potential.
  • Regularly updating the counseling column to provide the latest information on further education and employment.
  • Promoting pathways and preparation for further education and employment through activities, courses, and class (weekly) meetings.

(II) Life Counseling ~ Ensuring a Smooth Life for Students:

  • Understanding students' adaptation problems at school through mentorship surveys and providing assistance.
  • Discussing life issues with students through group counseling and individual counseling to help them solve problems.
  • Providing short counseling articles to establish appropriate life values.
  • Conducting orientation counseling to enhance life adaptation skills.
  • Assisting in establishing appropriate leisure concepts and engaging in beneficial physical and mental recreational activities.

(III) Parent Education ~ Letting Parents and the School Jointly Assist Students:

  • Asking homeroom teachers to contact parents to understand the student's living situation at home and to let parents know about their child's learning situation at school.
  • Holding parent education seminars to facilitate communication, exchange, and discussion among parents, students, and the school.
  • Providing parenting consultation for parents to enhance their knowledge in education.

(IV) Consultation and Referral ~ Helping Parents and Students Access More Resources:

  • Collecting and organizing various social resources to provide a reference for consultation services.
  • Collaborating with social counseling agencies, such as the Yilan County Student Counseling and Consultation Center, the "Teacher Chang" Center, the Yilan County Health Bureau, psychiatrists, and the Social Affairs Bureau, to provide consultation and referral services.
  • Hiring a school-based psychologist to provide student counseling, teacher consultation, and parent counseling services.

(V) Committees:

  • Student Counseling Work Committee.
  • Life Education and Family Education Promotion and Execution Team.
  • Student Grievance and Re-appeal Review Committee.
  • School's Youth Education and Employment Savings Account Execution Team.

Accounting and Statistics Office

Current Situation:

The office currently has one director, one staff member, and one assistant, for a total of 3 people.

Main Business:

The accounting work of this office is carried out under the current laws and regulations, in line with the needs of the school's business development. It handles the preparation of the annual budget, the control of income and expenditure funds, internal audits, and accounting processing. It also uses professional accounting knowledge for coordination and communication to provide the best professional services to assist the school in promoting its affairs and achieving its administrative goals.

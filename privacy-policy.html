5. Data Security Measures

TCVS implements comprehensive technical, administrative, and physical security measures to protect personal information and data from unauthorized access, use, disclosure, alteration, and destruction. Our security framework is designed to address evolving cyber threats and maintain the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information systems and data.

5.1 Technical Security Controls

We employ robust technical security controls across our digital infrastructure, including secure socket layer (SSL) encryption and transport layer security (TLS) protocols for data transmission; strong encryption algorithms for data storage and backup; multi-factor authentication for access to sensitive systems and information; firewalls and intrusion detection/prevention systems to monitor and block unauthorized access attempts; regular security patches and updates for operating systems, applications, and software; anti-malware and anti-virus protection software deployed across all systems; secure configuration of servers, databases, and network devices; regular vulnerability assessments and penetration testing; security information and event management (SIEM) systems for monitoring and analyzing security events; data loss prevention (DLP) technologies to prevent unauthorized data exfiltration; and continuous monitoring of network traffic and system activities for suspicious behavior.

5.2 Administrative and Organizational Controls

Our administrative security measures include clearly defined information security policies and procedures governing data handling practices; role-based access controls limiting access to personal information based on job responsibilities and the principle of least privilege; comprehensive background checks for employees with access to sensitive information; mandatory information security awareness training for all staff members; confidentiality agreements and non-disclosure obligations for employees and contractors; regular internal audits and compliance reviews to ensure adherence to security policies; incident response plans and procedures for addressing security breaches; designated data protection officer responsible for overseeing privacy and security compliance; privacy impact assessments for new systems and processes involving personal data; vendor management processes to ensure third-party compliance with security requirements; clear data retention and disposal policies; and ongoing evaluation and improvement of security practices in response to emerging threats and technological changes.

5.3 Physical Security Measures

Physical security measures protect our information systems and data storage facilities, including restricted access to server rooms and data centers with electronic access controls; surveillance cameras monitoring critical infrastructure areas; environmental controls to protect against fire, flood, and other physical threats; secure disposal procedures for paper records and electronic storage media; locked storage for backup media and archived records; visitor management and identification procedures for individuals entering secure areas; alarm systems and 24/7 monitoring of critical facilities; regular inspection and maintenance of physical security systems; off-site backup storage in secure, geographically distributed locations; and emergency power supply systems to ensure continuous operation of critical systems.

6. Data Retention and Disposal

TCVS maintains personal information only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected, comply with legal and regulatory requirements, and serve legitimate educational interests. Our data retention practices are guided by applicable laws, professional standards, and institutional policies.

Student academic records, including transcripts, grades, and enrollment information, are typically retained permanently to provide lifelong access to educational credentials and support alumni services. Other student information, such as disciplinary records, attendance records, and routine correspondence, may be retained for specific periods ranging from several years to the duration of enrollment plus a defined retention period. Employment records for faculty and staff are maintained according to employment law requirements and institutional policies. Financial records are retained in accordance with accounting standards and tax regulations. Website logs and technical data are typically retained for shorter periods necessary for system administration and security monitoring purposes.

When information is no longer needed and the retention period has expired, we implement secure disposal procedures to ensure complete destruction of data. Paper records are shredded or incinerated using certified destruction services. Electronic data is permanently deleted using secure erasure methods that prevent recovery, or storage media is physically destroyed when appropriate. Disposal activities are documented and audited to ensure compliance with data protection requirements and institutional policies.

16. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

Our website uses cookies and similar tracking technologies to enhance user experience, analyze website performance, and support various functionalities. This section provides detailed information about the types of cookies we use, their purposes, and how you can manage your cookie preferences.

16.1 Types of Cookies We Use

Essential Cookies: These cookies are strictly necessary for the operation of our website and cannot be disabled in our systems. They are usually set in response to actions you take, such as setting your privacy preferences, logging in, or filling in forms. Essential cookies enable core functionalities such as security, network management, and accessibility. Without these cookies, services you have requested cannot be provided. You can set your browser to block or alert you about these cookies, but some parts of the website may not function properly as a result.

Performance and Analytics Cookies: These cookies allow us to count visits and traffic sources, measure and improve the performance of our website, and understand which pages are most and least popular. They help us know how visitors move around the site and identify areas where improvements can be made. All information collected by these cookies is aggregated and therefore anonymous. Performance cookies collect data such as page load times, server response times, error messages, and user interaction patterns. This information helps us optimize website speed, identify technical issues, and improve overall site functionality. If you do not allow these cookies, we will not know when you visited our site and will not be able to monitor its performance effectively.

Functional Cookies: These cookies enable enhanced functionality and personalization features on our website. They may be set by us or by third-party providers whose services we have added to our pages. Functional cookies remember choices you make, such as your preferred language, region, username, or customization settings. They help provide enhanced, more personalized features and improve your experience on our website. If you do not allow these cookies, some or all of these services may not function properly, and you may need to re-enter information or reset preferences during each visit.

Session Cookies: Session cookies are temporary cookies that remain in your browser only during your visit to our website and are automatically deleted when you close your browser. These cookies are essential for maintaining your session state, preserving your login status, and ensuring continuity as you navigate through different pages of our website. Session cookies do not collect information from your device and do not track your browsing activities on other websites.

Persistent Cookies: Persistent cookies remain on your device for a set period specified in the cookie or until you manually delete them. These cookies help our website remember your preferences and settings across multiple visits, such as language preferences, display settings, or login credentials. Persistent cookies improve user convenience by eliminating the need to re-enter information during subsequent visits and enable us to provide a more personalized experience based on your previous interactions with our website.

16.2 Third-Party Cookies and Services

Our website may use third-party services that set their own cookies on your device. These third-party cookies are subject to the respective privacy policies of these external services, not our Privacy Policy. Third-party services we may use include web analytics providers such as Google Analytics, which help us understand website traffic patterns and user behavior; content delivery networks that improve website loading speed and performance; social media integration services that enable sharing and interaction with social platforms; and embedded content providers such as video hosting services. You can manage cookie preferences through your browser settings, which typically offer options to block all cookies, block third-party cookies only, delete cookies when you close your browser, or create exceptions for specific websites. 17. Data Processing and Transfer Mechanisms

Understanding how personal information flows through our systems and the safeguards we implement during processing and transfer is essential for transparency and accountability. This section provides detailed information about our data processing activities, transfer mechanisms, and protection measures.

17.1 Data Processing Principles

TCVS processes personal information in accordance with fundamental data protection principles that guide all our data handling activities. We adhere to the principle of lawfulness, fairness, and transparency, ensuring that we process data only when we have a valid legal basis and communicate our practices clearly to individuals. The principle of purpose limitation requires us to collect personal information for specified, explicit, and legitimate purposes and not process it in ways incompatible with those purposes. We follow the principle of data minimization, collecting only the personal information that is adequate, relevant, and necessary for the purposes for which it is processed. The principle of accuracy requires us to take reasonable steps to ensure personal information is accurate and kept up to date, and we promptly correct or delete inaccurate data upon identification or request.

We observe the principle of storage limitation, retaining personal information only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected or as required by law, after which we securely delete or anonymize the information. The principle of integrity and confidentiality obligates us to process personal information in a manner that ensures appropriate security, including protection against unauthorized or unlawful processing and against accidental loss, destruction, or damage. Finally, the principle of accountability requires us to take responsibility for complying with data protection principles and demonstrate our compliance through documentation, policies, and regular assessments.

17.2 Legal Basis for Processing

We process personal information only when we have a valid legal basis under applicable data protection laws. The legal bases for our processing activities include consent, where individuals have given clear and informed consent for us to process their personal information for specific purposes; contractual necessity, where processing is necessary for the performance of a contract with the individual or to take steps at the individual's request before entering into a contract; legal obligation, where processing is necessary for compliance with legal obligations to which TCVS is subject; vital interests, where processing is necessary to protect the vital interests of individuals or others; public task, where processing is necessary for the performance of tasks carried out in the public interest or in the exercise of official authority vested in TCVS as an educational institution; and legitimate interests, where processing is necessary for the legitimate interests pursued by TCVS or third parties, provided such interests are not overridden by the fundamental rights and freedoms of individuals.

For students under the age of 18, we recognize that additional protections apply, and we typically rely on legal bases such as parental consent, contractual necessity for educational services, or legitimate interests that align with the best interests of the child. We ensure that our processing activities for children's data are conducted with enhanced safeguards and transparency, and we respect the rights of parents and guardians to oversee the processing of their children's personal information.

17.3 Cross-Border Data Transfers

In today's interconnected digital environment, personal information may occasionally be transferred across international borders to facilitate our operations, utilize cloud services, or collaborate with international partners. When such transfers occur, TCVS implements appropriate safeguards to ensure that personal information receives adequate protection regardless of where it is processed or stored. We conduct transfer impact assessments to evaluate the adequacy of data protection in destination countries, considering factors such as local privacy laws, government surveillance practices, and available legal remedies for individuals.

Our safeguards for international data transfers include selecting service providers and partners in countries recognized as providing adequate data protection standards; implementing standard contractual clauses approved by relevant data protection authorities that establish binding obligations on data recipients; utilizing binding corporate rules for transfers within affiliated organizations; obtaining explicit consent from individuals for specific transfers when appropriate; ensuring that transfers are necessary for important public interests or for the establishment, exercise, or defense of legal claims; and conducting regular reviews of international transfer arrangements to ensure ongoing compliance with evolving legal requirements and best practices.

18. Automated Decision-Making and Profiling

TCVS is committed to transparency regarding any automated decision-making processes or profiling activities that may affect individuals. As an educational institution, our use of automated processing is limited and primarily focused on administrative efficiency and educational quality improvement.

18.1 Limited Use of Automated Processing

We may use automated systems for certain administrative tasks such as initial screening of admissions applications based on objective criteria, automatic grade calculation and GPA computation, scheduling algorithms that assign students to classes based on preferences and availability, and automated alerts for attendance patterns or academic performance concerns that may require intervention. These automated processes are designed to support human decision-making rather than replace it entirely. Significant decisions affecting students, such as admissions outcomes, disciplinary actions, or academic progression, always involve human review and judgment by qualified educators and administrators.

When automated systems are used, we ensure they operate based on fair and transparent criteria, are regularly reviewed for accuracy and bias, include mechanisms for human oversight and intervention, and respect individuals' rights to challenge automated decisions and request human review. We do not engage in automated decision-making that produces legal effects or similarly significantly affects individuals without appropriate safeguards, human involvement, and the right to appeal.

18.2 Educational Analytics and Profiling

We may conduct educational analytics using aggregated or pseudonymized student data to identify trends, evaluate program effectiveness, and improve educational outcomes. Such analytics may involve analyzing patterns in academic performance, course completion rates, engagement levels, or progression pathways to inform curriculum development, identify students who may benefit from additional support, and allocate resources effectively. These analytics activities are conducted in accordance with ethical principles, respect for individual privacy, and compliance with applicable laws.

When conducting analytics that could be considered profiling, we implement safeguards including anonymization or pseudonymization of data whenever possible to prevent identification of individuals; aggregation of data to analyze group trends rather than individual characteristics; limitation of access to analytics results to authorized personnel with legitimate educational purposes; transparent communication with students and families about how data is used for educational improvement; and respect for individuals' rights to understand, question, and object to profiling activities. We do not use profiling for discriminatory purposes or in ways that could unfairly disadvantage individuals or groups.

Certain types of personal information are considered particularly sensitive and require enhanced protection measures. TCVS handles special categories of personal information with the utmost care and only when necessary for legitimate educational purposes.

19.1 Definition and Examples

Special categories of personal information, also known as sensitive personal data, include information that reveals or relates to racial or ethnic origin; religious or philosophical beliefs; political opinions; trade union membership; genetic data; biometric data used for identification purposes; physical or mental health information; information about sexual orientation or sexual life; and criminal conviction records or information about alleged offenses. In an educational context, we may process certain special categories of information such as health information relevant to providing appropriate educational accommodations or medical care, information about learning disabilities or special educational needs, dietary restrictions or preferences related to religious or health requirements, and emergency medical information necessary for student safety.

19.2 Enhanced Protection Measures

When processing special categories of personal information, we implement enhanced protection measures including obtaining explicit consent from individuals or their legal guardians when required by law; limiting processing to situations where it is necessary for substantial public interest in education, health or safety reasons, or compliance with legal obligations; restricting access to sensitive information to authorized personnel with specific need and appropriate training; applying additional technical and organizational security measures such as encryption, access logging, and segregated storage; conducting privacy impact assessments before initiating new processing activities involving sensitive data; and providing individuals with clear information about why we process sensitive information and their rights regarding such processing. We regularly review our handling of special categories of personal information to ensure compliance with legal requirements and alignment with best practices for protecting sensitive data.

20.1 Right to Be Informed

You have the right to be informed about the collection and use of your personal information. This Privacy Policy serves as our primary means of fulfilling this obligation by providing clear and comprehensive information about our data practices. We ensure that information is provided in a concise, transparent, intelligible, and easily accessible form, using clear and plain language. When collecting personal information directly from you, we provide information about our identity and contact details, the purposes for which we process the information, the legal basis for processing, with whom we share the information, and your rights regarding the information. When we obtain personal information from other sources, we provide similar information within a reasonable period, typically within one month.

20.2 Right to Rectification and Erasure

You have the right to have inaccurate personal information corrected and incomplete personal information completed. If you identify errors or omissions in the information we hold about you, please notify us, and we will promptly investigate and make necessary corrections. In certain circumstances, you also have the right to erasure of your personal information, sometimes referred to as the "right to be forgotten." This right applies when personal information is no longer necessary for the purposes for which it was collected; you withdraw consent on which processing is based and there is no other legal ground for processing; you object to processing and there are no overriding legitimate grounds for continued processing; personal information has been unlawfully processed; or erasure is required to comply with a legal obligation. However, the right to erasure is not absolute, and we may refuse requests when processing is necessary for compliance with legal obligations, for reasons of public interest in education, for the establishment, exercise, or defense of legal claims, or for archiving purposes in the public interest.

20.3 Right to Restriction and Objection

You have the right to restrict processing of your personal information in certain circumstances, such as when you contest the accuracy of the information and we need time to verify accuracy; the processing is unlawful but you prefer restriction over erasure; we no longer need the information but you require it for legal claims; or you have objected to processing and we need to verify whether our legitimate grounds override your interests. When processing is restricted, we may only store the information and must inform you before lifting the restriction. You also have the right to object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing purposes. When you object to processing for direct marketing, we will cease such processing immediately. For objections based on your particular situation, we will cease processing unless we can demonstrate compelling legitimate grounds that override your interests, rights, and freedoms, or the processing is necessary for legal claims.

20.4 Right to Data Portability

You have the right to receive personal information you have provided to us in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format and to transmit that information to another organization without hindrance. This right applies when processing is based on consent or contract and is carried out by automated means. Where technically feasible, you can request that we transmit the information directly to another organization. The right to data portability complements your rights to access and erasure but does not extend to all personal information we hold about youonly to information you have actively provided to us.
